Connect your aviation headset to your simulator

Practice with the same headset you fly with

Save Money With Only One Headset

Why spend extra money on a gaming headset when you can get more familiar with the incredible (and expensive) headset you already have. Building a computer specific for your simulator? Forego the cost of both the sound card and second headset with this adapter and spend the extra on a better graphics card. Mount the adapter within easy reach - unlike competitive products that are more expensive and leave wires hanging around.

Also works with iPads / iPhones / Android Devices

In addition to working with Mac, Windows, and Linux, we also work when connected to iPhones, iPads, and Android devices with USB Type C ports. Flying commercial, but taking your GA headset with you on vacation? You can use it to movies or games on the go. We know you are secretly hoping they also come over the intercom to ask: "Is there another pilot on board?" - so you'll be ready to go too.

Preflight Your Headset Configuration

Everything has gotten more high tech, including your headset. Your preflight should start at home. Verify your bluetooth connections to your iPad and phone are correct, test notifications from ForeFlight are working, make sure your hours flying straight and level won't have you troubleshooting why your phone is not connecting, and of course practice on VatSim/IVAO/PilotEdge with your own headset.

Powered by USB

General Aviation headsets require power for the microphone to work. 9er Systems' GA2USB uses power from the USB port to enable the microphone without the need for batteries.

Designed, manufactured, and supported in the USA

Designed and supported by engineers in Lexington KY and Knoxville TN, manufactured and assembled in Asheville NC. You spent a lot of money on your headset, you should feel confident that the devices you connect them to will keep them safe with high design and manufacturing standards.

Quality Assurance from the owners

The owners of 9er Systems use GA2USB with their own headsets. They have tested and actively use it with their personal Bose A20's, Lightspeed Zulu3's, David Clark ONE-X's, Rugged Radios, and some 30+ year old FlightCom's. Whether you have ANR or not, the 9er Systems GA2USB device is safe and compatible.

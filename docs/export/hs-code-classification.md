## Product Description

The USB2GA is a USB-powered electronic adapter that connects general aviation (GA) dual-plug headsets (6.35mm/0.250" jacks for speakers and microphone) to computers, tablets, and mobile devices via USB-C. Key characteristics:

- Contains a DAC/ADC chip for analog-to-digital audio conversion
- Provides impedance matching for GA headsets (300 ohm speakers)
- Supplies 12V phantom power to aviation electret condenser microphones
- Powered entirely via USB (no batteries)
- Cannot function independently — requires connection to a host computer/tablet/phone
- All audio processing occurs on the host device; the USB2GA serves as an input/output peripheral
- Plug and Play compatible with Windows, Mac, Linux, Android, iOS
- Dimensions: 2" L x 4" W x 1.5" H
- Packaging: 7" L x 5" W x 2" H
- UPC/GTIN: 00198168276327
- Engineered, Produced, and Supported in the USA (designed in KY/TN, manufactured/assembled in NC)

## How These Codes Were Determined

### Step 1: Initial Classification Analysis

The USB2GA was evaluated against multiple HS headings across Chapters 84 and 85. Candidates included:

| HS Heading | Description | Fit Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| **8471.80** | Other units of automatic data processing machines | **Selected** — USB2GA is a dependent peripheral unit for ADP machines, matching CBP precedent for audio interface devices |
| 8543.70 | Electrical machines/apparatus with individual functions, NESOI | Rejected — too many layers of "Other" catch-alls; less descriptive |
| 8517.62 | Machines for reception/conversion/transmission of voice/data | Rejected — heading 8517 is oriented toward telecom/networking equipment |
| 8517.79 | Parts of voice/data transmission apparatus | Rejected — USB2GA is a complete device, not a "part"; also 8517.70 no longer exists in 2026 Schedule B |
| 8504.40 | Static converters (rectifiers, etc.) | Rejected — power conversion is secondary function |
| 8518 | Microphones, loudspeakers, headphones, audio amplifiers | Rejected — adapter is not itself audio reproduction equipment |
| 8542.39 | Electronic integrated circuits (ADCs/DACs) | Rejected — 8542 covers bare IC chips, not finished assembled products |
| 8471.80.1000 | Control and adaptor units | Considered — descriptive name but no CBP precedent for audio interfaces under this specific code |

### Step 2: CBP Ruling Precedent — N125536 (Primary Basis)

A binding CBP ruling **N125536** (dated October 22, 2010) was identified as directly on-point precedent. This ruling classified **HD I/O Audio Interface Devices** from China — professional audio interfaces that convert analog audio signals to/from digital format for computer processing.

**CBP classified these devices under 8471.80.9000** with the following reasoning:

> The audio interface devices function as "input/output interfaces for automated data processing machines." They convert analog audio signals to digital format for computer processing. "The audio interface devices cannot transmit or receive audio when disconnected from the ADP machine." All mixing, editing, and storage occurs on the connected computer, not within the interface device itself.

**Why the USB2GA matches this precedent exactly:**
- Like the HD I/O devices, the USB2GA converts analog audio signals (from aviation headset) to digital format (USB) for processing by a computer
- The USB2GA cannot function independently — it requires a host computer, tablet, or phone to operate
- All audio processing (flight simulator audio, VoIP communication, etc.) occurs on the host device
- The USB2GA is a dependent peripheral unit, not a standalone audio device
- Both products are external USB audio interfaces that serve as I/O peripherals for automatic data processing machines

**Duty rate per precedent: Free**

### Step 3: Supporting CBP Ruling — N150796

An earlier ruling **N150796** (March 29, 2011) classified a passive audio & video adapter under **8517.70.0000**. While initially considered, this code was rejected because:
- 8517.70.0000 **no longer exists** in the 2026 Schedule B (restructured into 8517.71 and 8517.79)
- The ruled-upon adapter was a passive device (RCA jacks only), unlike the USB2GA's active electronics
- Heading 8517 is oriented toward telecom/networking, not computer peripherals

### Step 4: Supporting CBP Ruling — HQ 083967

An older ruling **HQ 083967** (June 21, 1989) classified a standalone DAC (digital-to-analog converter with headphone amplifier) under **8543.80.9080** (electrical machines with individual functions, NESOI). This was rejected for the USB2GA because:
- That product was a standalone device that could operate independently
- The USB2GA is a dependent computer peripheral, making 8471.80 more appropriate per N125536

### Step 5: Argentina NCM Code

Argentina uses the NCM (Nomenclatura Comun del Mercosur) tariff schedule, which shares the first 6 digits with the international HS code system. The corresponding classification is **8471.80.90** ("Other" at the Mercosur level under the same heading).

## Tax Collection Obligations (US Seller)

The seller (US LLC) has **no obligation to collect taxes** on this export sale:

- **US Sales Tax** — Exports are exempt from state sales tax
- **US Export Duties** — The US does not impose export duties
- **Argentine Taxes** — Collected by Argentine customs (ARCA) from the buyer/importer at delivery, not by the seller

**Seller's only obligation:** Accurately declare the sale price and product description on the commercial invoice accompanying the shipment. Undervaluing to reduce the buyer's tax burden is illegal in both countries.

## Argentine Import Taxes and Duties (Buyer's Responsibility)

> **IMPORTANT: These rates are as of March 2026 and are subject to change.** The US-Argentina trade landscape is actively evolving. Verify current rates with a customs broker or ARCA before shipping.

The following taxes are assessed by Argentine customs (ARCA) on the **CIF value** (Cost + Insurance + Freight) and are paid by the buyer/importer:

| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Import Duty (Derecho de Importacion)** | 0–16% (varies by NCM code) | For NCM 8471.80.90 (computer peripherals), typically in the 0-16% range. Confirm exact rate via ARCA integrated tariff tool. |
| **IVA (VAT)** | 21% | Argentina's standard VAT rate. Applied on CIF value + import duty. |
| **IVA Adicional (Advance VAT Perception)** | 20% (individuals) / 10% (registered businesses) | Additional advance VAT withholding for importers. |
| **Statistics Fee (Tasa de Estadistica)** | 3% | Capped at USD $150,000. Applied on CIF value. |
| **Impuesto PAIS** | **Eliminated** | Expired December 23, 2024. Was not renewed for 2026. |
| **Income Tax Perception** | 30% (may apply) | Withholding on foreign currency purchases; can be credited against income tax by the buyer. |

**Estimated total tax burden for the buyer:** Could be 50%+ on top of the declared CIF value, depending on the specific import duty rate for NCM 8471.80.90. Recommend alerting the buyer to expect significant customs charges at delivery.

### Recent Changes Affecting These Rates (as of March 2026)

1. **Impuesto PAIS — Eliminated.** This 30% surcharge on foreign currency transactions expired on December 23, 2024 under the Milei administration and was not renewed. This significantly reduces the buyer's total import cost compared to prior years.

2. **US-Argentina ARTI Trade Agreement** — Signed February 5, 2026 between Presidents Trump and Milei. Argentina committed to cutting tariffs on many US goods including "information technology products" and "machinery." However:
   - The agreement requires Argentine Congressional approval (submitted for consideration March 2026)
   - The US Supreme Court struck down Trump's IEEPA-based tariff authority on February 20, 2026, which may affect the legal basis for the US side of the deal
   - Trump responded with a 10% global tariff under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974
   - The agreement's implementation status is uncertain as of this writing

3. **US Export Tariffs** — The US does not impose export duties. The Supreme Court ruling and subsequent tariff changes affect US *import* tariffs, not exports. Your shipment to Argentina is not affected by US tariff policy.

**Bottom line for this shipment:** The US export side is straightforward (no export duties). The Argentine import side carries significant taxes. The exact rates should be confirmed with the buyer's customs broker or via ARCA's integrated tariff tool at https://www.afip.gob.ar/aduana/arancelIntegrado/ using NCM code 8471.80.90.
